Curse of the Corsair

Curse of the Corsair is a 2D action-adventure game made as a case study of Hyper Light Drifter by Heart Machine. It was developed in Unity and C# during the 2023 winter semester by WolverineSoft Studio: the student-led game development organization at the University of Michigan. I worked with a team of around ~35 other designers, artists, programmers, producers, marketers, and sound designers throughout this project. We utilize the Agile design process, along with tools such as Jira, Confluence, and Bitbucket to streamline our development process.

Curse of the Corsair was my first time working as a game designer with WolverineSoft. I was assigned to be the game's enemy designer, so naturally I worked closely with the player designer to develop a satisfying combat loop. Then I wrote design documentation for the three regular enemies, two level bosses, and the final boss that I had designed. The documentation is very thorough, it includes attacks(complete with timing, diagrams, and damage values), behavior trees, use cases, and a written explanation of what strategy a player might employ to defeat the enemy. In addition to iterating on those designs, I utilized my Unity experience to bring implementation in line with my designs and fix bugs. My prior experience as a programmer on Subtension also allowed me to communicate my needs effectively with the programming team, which kept my squad running like a well-oiled machine!

I'm very proud of my design for the final boss. I was told that I'd need to design a final boss for the game about a week after I had turned in my design for the boss of the second level, and by that point, we knew that art wouldn't be able to make many more new assets. I thought for a while about how to make a climactic and intimidating final boss without asking art for a whole bunch of new assets. This was a really difficult problem, but I really like the solution I came up with! And I think that the behavior of the boss makes it super aggressive and fun to fight, even for me, and I had to fight it a lot of times to tune the timing and damage values.

Subtension

Subtension is a real-time strategy game made as a case study of FTL:Faster Than Light by Subset Games. It was developed in Unity and C# over the 2022 summer and fall semesters by WolverineSoft Studio: the student-led game development organization at the University of Michigan. I worked with a team of around ~45 other programmers, artists, designers, producers, marketers, and sound designers throughout this project. We utilize the Agile design process, along with tools such as Jira, Confluence, and Bitbucket to streamline our development process.

I worked on Subtension as a programmer in both the summer and fall semesters. In the summer I wrote documentation in confluence so that we could onboard the new programmers in the fall. We also created the combat prototype for the game, which can be downloaded on WolverineSoft's itch.io page below. During the prototyping I worked on most aspects of the game, but my primary focus was creating the crew members, as well as implementing crew pathfinding. In the fall I fleshed out those systems and helped to apply a level of polish across the entire game. Additionally, Subtension marked the start of WolverineSoft publishing our games on Steam!

Moles, Voles, and Trolls

Moles, Voles, and Trolls is a tower-stacker game where you dig up building materials in the dig phase, and then stack them as high as you can to get a high score. I made the project in Unity with 4 other people as a submission for WolverineSoft's 2021 Turkey Game Jam. The theme for the jam was, "Making a mountain out of a molehill". My team and I ended up coming in 1st place with this project!

I was heavily involved from the get-go, as both one of the designers of the game and chief programmers. I'm still very proud of this jam project, I think it turned out to be really polished and fun!

Overdeveloped

Overdeveloped is a multiplayer-strategy game based off of the games Overcooked by Team17 and Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes by Steel Crate Games. In the game, you play the role of a game developer who must create games based on the cryptic hints given from your contractors. You'll need to use the manual to decipher the hints, make the correct development decisions, and earn as much money as possible before your studio goes bankrupt!.

I worked on the project as both a designer and programmer. I created it in Unity3D with 3 other people as a submission for WolverineSoft's 2023 Shamrock Game Jam. The theme for the jam was, "From Scratch". Overdeveloped ended up winning second place in the jam, as well as the award for best music, sfx, and audio!
